Background

Growing up in a family deeply involved in motorcycle racing, my childhood was profoundly shaped by motorsport; I practically learned to ride a bike before I could walk. I began competing in official races at the age of six and continued to do so for the next ten years. Spending my youth in this environment allowed me to truly grasp the demand, discipline, and mindset required in motorsport; until, unfortunately, I had to step away from competition.

It was at that moment I realised that, although motocross had defined my childhood, my true passion lay not just in riding, but in understanding and developing the engineering behind racing vehicles. The seed was already sown, and I knew I couldn't walk away from this world.

This led me to find a new path to dedicate my life to motorsport: Engineering. I chose to pursue a BSc in Mechanical Engineering at the Universitat Politècnica de València, where I joined the FSUPV Team Academy. During my bachelor studies, I had a special focus on motorsport related modules, including composite materials, vehicle dynamics, CAD design, or Finite Element Analysis (FEA).

My final degree project was also automotive-oriented. I was responsible for the calibration and validation of an autonomous driving simulator, which is currently used to study the psychological impact of autonomous driving on drivers in different conditions.

Seeking a more intensive and specialised education, I decided to push myself out of my comfort zone and move to the UK to study the MSc in Motorsport Engineering at Oxford Brookes University. There, I joined the Formula Student team (OBR), contributing to the Fluid Dynamics department with a focus on the design of the car's side wings. To date, I have completed the first semester with top marks, achieving a Distinction grade in both Advanced Vehicle Dynamics and Advanced Vehicle Aerodynamics.

Now that I have built a solid technical foundation, I am seeking opportunities to transition into the professional world of motorsport and contribute to the development of racing vehicles at the highest level.
